# Key Technology

# Phase-Variance Shields
## Core Principle
Phase-variance fields manipulate spacetime to shift objects slightly out of normal phase, making them unable to interact with conventional matter while remaining visible to other phased objects. This technology was discovered by Dr. James Adeyemi through studying an artifact of unknown origin found on a barren planet.

## Technical Specifications
**How It Works:**
- Generates localized spacetime distortion around target object
- Shifts object's quantum phase relative to normal spacetime
- Creates state where object exists "alongside" but not "within" normal reality
- Phased objects can pass through normal matter without resistance
- Energy barrier between phases prevents interaction

**Power Requirements:** 

Power consumption scales with mass—larger objects require exponentially more energy to phase:

- Personal scale (human-sized, ~100kg): 2-5 kW continuous for hours of operation
- Shuttle scale (~20,000kg): 500 kW-1 MW for 15-30 minutes
- Small craft (~100,000kg): 5-10 MW for minutes
- Capital ships (millions of kg): Power requirements exceed even massive reactor output—impossible with current technology

**Duration Capabilities by Era:**

*Act I (Prototype, 90 seconds):*
- Single-use generators with limited power cells
- 90-second fixed duration before burnout
- Barely understood principles
- Temperamental and unpredictable
- No recharge capability

*Act II (Refined, 3-8 minutes):*
- Personal shields: 3-5 minutes operational
- Shuttle shields: 5-8 minutes operational
- Improved power management and efficiency
- More reliable phase transition
- Still single-charge but better quality

*Act III (Mature, hours theoretical/20-30min practical):*
- Personal shields: Hours theoretical duration
- Practical limit: 20-30 minutes due to psychological factors
- Shuttle shields: 15-30 minutes operational
- Small devices (mines, drones): Hours to days possible
- Recharge capability developed but slow

**Theoretical Maximum:**

With sufficient power, phase-shields could theoretically operate indefinitely. The practical limits are:

- Psychological strain on organic users (see below)
- Power generation/storage capacity
- Equipment heat dissipation
- Structural stress on phase generators

## Critical Limitations
**1. Psychological Strain:**

Extended phasing creates cumulative effects on human consciousness:

- 0-5 minutes: Initial disorientation, nausea, spatial confusion
- 5-15 minutes: Adaptation phase, veterans functional by 10 seconds, rookies need 15-20 seconds
- 15-30 minutes: Increased disorientation even for veterans, performance degradation
- 30-60 minutes: Severe psychological effects, hallucinations possible, dangerous even for exceptional individuals
- 60+ minutes: Extreme danger—complete dissociation, permanent psychological damage possible

Training alleviates but never eliminates strain. Individual tolerance varies dramatically (see Phase Tolerance section).

**2. Communication Blackout:**

- Radio waves cannot penetrate phase boundary
- Electronic communications completely impossible
- Physical sound transmission works if phased objects touch
- Visual light passes between phased objects normally
- Led to development of Visible Light Communication (VLC) systems

**3. Complete Non-Interaction:**

Phased objects cannot meaningfully interact with normal spacetime:

- Cannot fire weapons (bullets/energy don't transition)
- Cannot operate equipment requiring calibration or adjustment
- Cannot open doors, push objects, or manipulate anything
- Cannot be seen by normal sensors (thermal, electromagnetic, visual)
- Cannot be detected by conventional means

**4. Contact-Based Deactivation:**

Objects phase with the generator but deactivate based on contact:

- Items held/worn by phased person remain phased
- Breaking physical contact = immediate materialization (1-2 second transition)
- Dropped items become visible and cannot be retrieved while phased
- Creates tactical discipline requirement—don't drop anything critical

**5. Energy Detection (Unexploited):**

Phased objects may emit exotic energy signatures detectable by specialized sensors. The Krell haven't yet developed detection methods, but theoretically:

- Neutrino emissions might be abnormal
- Quantum entanglement patterns might be detectable
- Gravitational microdistortions might register
- This remains an unexploited vulnerability the Confederation worries about.

## Phase Tolerance (Individual Variation)

Human tolerance to phase-variance effects varies significantly across populations:

**Severe Intolerance (30% of screened population):**

- Performance degrades catastrophically within 5-7 minutes
- Extreme nausea, disorientation, inability to function
- Cannot serve in boarding teams
- May serve in rear-echelon or support roles

**Adequate Tolerance (50% of screened population):**

- Functional for 5-10 minutes before degradation
- Can serve as combat engineers (phase in, materialize quickly, assemble anchors)
- Can serve as shuttle pilots (single brief phase per mission)
- Valuable specialists but not frontline boarders

**Good Tolerance (20% of screened population):**

- Functional for 15-20 minutes with manageable degradation
- Qualify as boarding specialists
- Form core of Shield Doctrine forces
- Can execute complex multi-phase operations

**Exceptional Tolerance (1% of screened population):**

- Functional for 30-90 minutes with minimal degradation
- Elite specialists for extended reconnaissance
- Deep infiltration missions
- Special operations requiring prolonged phasing

**Training Effects:**

- Repeated exposure improves adaptation speed (veterans functional in 5-10 seconds vs. 15-20 for rookies)
- Training cannot change fundamental tolerance category
- Focus on phase tolerance training diminishes other skills—must be balanced

**Career Path:**

Boarding teams require minimum "good tolerance" (20% category). Of those who qualify, only ~33% complete full boarding specialist training, creating ~6-7% of experienced legionaries as operational boarders.

## Tactical Applications
**Infiltration:**

- Walk through enemy hulls, walls, obstacles
- Position within enemy formations undetected
- Scout locations before committing forces
- Pre-position equipment and ambushes

**Surprise Attack:**

- Materialize at point-blank range
- Staggered materialization confuses enemy about numbers
- Bypass defensive lines entirely
- Attack from unexpected directions

**Equipment Delivery:**

- Carry heavy weapons, explosives, drones through obstacles
- Place equipment in optimal positions before fighting starts
- Deploy mines and traps in enemy-controlled areas
- Transport warp corridor components to assembly points

**Limitations in Combat:**

- Cannot fight while phased
- Must materialize to engage
- Psychological strain limits duration
- Communication restricted to VLC
- Requires extensive training for effectiveness

## Manufacturing and Distribution
**Production Complexity:**

Phase-variance generators are sophisticated devices requiring:

- Exotic materials for emitter arrays
- Precision manufacturing (sub-micron tolerances)
- Specialized power cells
- Quantum calibration equipment

**Cost:**

- Personal generator: ~50,000 credits (expensive but not prohibitive)
- Shuttle generator: ~2,000,000 credits
- Production time: 2-3 days per personal unit with proper facilities

**Supply Chain:**

By Act III, Confederation produces ~200 personal generators per week across multiple facilities. This limits how fast Shield Doctrine can scale—can't equip thousands of legionaries overnight.

## Future Development Possibilities
**Potential Improvements:**

- Longer duration through better power management
- Faster phase transition (reduce disorientation)
- Detection resistance (shield against exotic sensors)
- Multi-person field (one generator phases multiple users)
- Remote activation/deactivation

**Theoretical Applications:**

- Phase-shifted armor (brief phase pulses to deflect incoming fire)
- Phased sensor arrays (undetectable surveillance)
- Phase-drive propulsion (though highly speculative)
- Medical applications (phased surgery?)

**Unknown Possibilities:**

The artifact that inspired phase-variance shields suggests deeper applications exist. The mathematical connections to jump drives hint at unified principles not yet understood.


# Warp Corridors (Einstein-Rosen Bridges)
## Core Principle

Warp corridors create stable spatial bridges between two points by synchronizing phase-variance fields and applying jump-drive spatial compression. This unexpected synthesis suggests deeper connections between seemingly unrelated technologies.

  
## Technical Specifications
**How It Works:**

- Two anchor units establish synchronized phase-variance fields
- Jump-compression algorithms calculate optimal spatial fold
- Combined effect creates stable corridor through spacetime
- Corridor interior exists in phase-shifted subspace
- Objects passing through experience instantaneous transit
- Corridor maintained by continuous power from both anchors

**Physical Characteristics:**
- Diameter: 3 meters (current portable technology limit)
- Length: Variable—depends on spatial distance and gravitational conditions
- Appearance: Shimmering ring of energy at each endpoint
- Interior: Phased subspace tunnel, instantaneous subjective transit
- Stability: Binary—works perfectly or fails completely

**Power Requirements:**

Enormous continuous energy draw dependent on:

- Distance between endpoints
- Local gravitational conditions
- Corridor diameter
- Duration of operation

**Typical power consumption:**

- 100,000 km: 50 MW continuous
- 500,000 km: 200 MW continuous
- 1,000,000 km: 500+ MW continuous

## Anchor System
**Anchor Components (Modular Design):**

Each anchor consists of 24 discrete components:

- 6 emitter units (create phase-variance field)
- 8 power cores (provide energy and capacitance)
- 6 stabilization units (maintain spatial coherence)
- 4 calibration modules (sync with partner anchor)

**Component Characteristics:**

- Weight: 8-12 kg per component
- Total anchor weight: ~250 kg
- Carried by 20 legionaries (12kg each average)
- Assembled on-site under fire

**Assembly Requirements:**

Cannot Assemble While Phased: Anchor assembly requires sophisticated calibration instruments that only function in normal spacetime. Combat engineers must:

1.  Phase in with components
2. Materialize and establish defensive perimeter
3. Assemble anchor from components
4. Calibrate instruments to local gravitational conditions
5. Synchronize with partner anchor
6. Activate corridor

**Assembly Time:**

- Ideal conditions (skilled team, no combat): 3 minutes
- Typical combat conditions: 5 minutes
- Difficult conditions (heavy fire, casualties): 7+ minutes

**Vulnerability Window:**

The 3-7 minute assembly period is critically vulnerable. Engineers are exposed, focused on delicate work, unable to defend effectively. Requires boarding specialists to establish and hold defensive perimeter.

## Gravitational Constraints

Warp corridors are profoundly affected by gravitational fields—similar to jump drives but more sensitive:


**Carrier Positioning Requirements:**

- Carrier must maintain position within calculated tolerance (typically 5,000 km)
- Tolerance depends on local gravitational environment
- Stronger gravity wells = tighter tolerance required
- Carrier drift beyond tolerance = corridor failure

**Sudden Failure Mode:**

Corridors don't degrade gracefully. They either:

- Work perfectly (stable connection, safe transit)
- Fail completely (collapse instantly, cut off reinforcements)
- No gradual warning—binary success/failure.

**Emergency Repositioning:**

If carrier must move due to combat damage or tactical necessity:

- Corridor collapses immediately
- Forces already through remain (instantaneous transit means no one trapped mid-corridor)
- Carrier can attempt reconnection by returning to position
- Reconnection unreliable and time-consuming (5-15 minutes recalibration)
- Teams on far side must decide: wait for reconnection, dismantle and move, or guard anchor and continue mission

**Distance Effects:**

- Longer distances require exponentially more power
- Gravitational complexity increases with distance (more intervening masses)
- Maximum reliable range: ~1,000,000 km in clear space
- Near planets/stars: significantly reduced range

## Pre-Alignment Requirements

Anchor pairs cannot be programmed on the fly:

**Pre-Mission Calibration (Hours Required):**

- Each anchor pair must be precisely aligned before deployment
- Process requires 2-4 hours in laboratory conditions
- Alignment encodes: destination coordinates, gravitational compensation, power synchronization
- Cannot be changed during battle

**Mission Planning Implications:**

- Limited number of pre-aligned pairs constrains operations
- Must predict target anchor locations before battle
- If tactical situation changes, cannot easily retarget
- Stolen or damaged anchors cannot be quickly repurposed

**Reusability:**

Anchors can be recovered and realigned after missions:

- Complete programming wipe: 30 minutes
- Full recalibration: 2-4 hours
- Not practical during active operations
- Logistics planning must account for anchor turnover time

## Operational Success Rates

**Act III Statistics (Hammerfall Battle):**

- 48 anchor pairs deployed
- 36 achieved stable connection (75% success)
- 8 failed completely (anchor malfunction, assembly failure, carrier positioning)
- 4 partial connection (worked briefly, collapsed prematurely)

**Failure Modes:**

- Assembly interrupted (combat damage to calibration equipment)
- Component loss (critical part dropped/destroyed during assembly)
- Carrier drift (forced out of position by combat damage)
- Gravitational interference (unexpected mass/field interaction)
- Power failure (anchor loses power during operation)
- Synchronization loss (anchors lose coordination)

**Reliability Trajectory:**

- Act III: 75% success rate (experimental technology)
- 6 months post-Hammerfall: 82% (refined procedures)
- 12 months: 88% (better equipment, more training)
- 18 months: 90%+ (mature technology)

## Strategic Implications

**Force Multiplication:**

A single successful corridor can deploy 200+ legionaries in 60 seconds:

- First wave: 40 veterans with heavy weapons
- Second wave: 60 combat engineers with additional anchors
- Third wave: 100+ assault troops
- Total time: Under 90 seconds from activation

**Resource Constraint:**

Limited pre-aligned anchor pairs restrict simultaneous operations:

- 48 pairs = maximum 48 simultaneous corridors
- Manufacturing new pairs: 1-2 weeks per set
- Recalibration between missions: 2-4 hours
- Strategic planning must allocate limited resources

**Carrier Vulnerability:**

Carriers must maintain position to keep corridors functional:

- Makes carriers priority targets
- Requires dedicated escort forces
- If carrier destroyed/forced to withdraw, teams stranded
- Creates asymmetric vulnerability—enemy can target carriers to disrupt boarding operations

**Tactical Flexibility:**

Cannot improvise corridor positions mid-battle:

- Pre-mission planning critical
- If situation changes, anchors cannot be easily retargeted
- Success depends on accurate intelligence and prediction
- Encourages redundancy (multiple anchor pairs per target)

## Manufacturing and Distribution

**Production Complexity:**

Anchor pairs are sophisticated and expensive:

- Requires specialized facilities
- Components use exotic materials
- Precision calibration equipment needed
- Production time: 1-2 weeks per anchor pair

**Cost:**

- Anchor pair: ~10,000,000 credits (extremely expensive)
- Limits how many can be produced
- Strategic asset requiring careful allocation

**Supply Chain:**

By Act III, Confederation produces ~4-6 anchor pairs per week. This severely constrains how fast Shield Doctrine can expand—can't equip dozens of assault carriers overnight.

## Future Development Possibilities

**Potential Improvements:**

- Larger diameter (allow vehicles/equipment through)
- Longer range (multiple light-seconds)
- Reduced power consumption
- Faster assembly (2-minute protocols)
-  More reliable gravitational compensation
- Field recalibration capability

**Theoretical Applications:**

- Permanent warp gate installations (replacing jump drives for commerce)
- Evacuation corridors (rescue operations)
- Logistics networks (instant cargo transfer)
- Strategic mobility (deploy entire legions instantly)

**Unknown Possibilities:**

The mathematical connection between phase-variance, warp corridors, and jump drives suggests unified principles with unexplored applications.

# Jump Drives

## Core Principle

Jump drives compress space between two points, allowing near-instantaneous transit across interstellar distances. The Confederation reverse-engineered this technology from captured Krell vessels ~5 years before current setting.


## Technical Specifications

**How It Works:**

- Calculate precise gravitational environment of departure and destination
- Charge jump cells with enormous energy
- Activate spatial compression field
- "Jump" through compressed space to destination
- Decompress space and emerge at target

**Jump Process:**

- Subjective experience: Instantaneous
- Objective time: Picoseconds to microseconds
- No physical travel through intervening space
- Ship effectively ceases to exist in origin point and exists in destination point

**Power Requirements:**

Massive energy consumption scaling exponentially with distance:

- 1 light-year: ~100 TJ (terajoules)
- 10 light-years: ~1 PJ (petajoule)
- 100 light-years: ~10 PJ
- 1000 light-years: Impractical with current technology

## Jump Cells

**Function:**

Specialized containment systems storing and releasing enormous energy in controlled burst:

- Banks of exotic matter reservoirs
- Magnetic containment fields
- Quantum tunneling release mechanisms
- Thermal management systems

**Capabilities:**

- Store energy equivalent to small fusion reactor output
- Release in microseconds for jump activation
- Can be overloaded beyond design specifications (dangerous)
- Recharge time: Hours to days depending on reactor output

**Lifespan:**

- Normal use: 100-200 jumps before replacement needed
- Overloaded use: May destroy cells immediately
- Manufacturing complex and expensive
- Maintenance critical—failure catastrophic

## Calculation Requirements

**Precision Mathematics:**

Jump accuracy depends entirely on calculation quality:

*Perfect Jump (hours to days calculation):*

- Map all significant gravitational sources
- Account for stellar motion over time
- Calculate spatial compression vectors
- Verify through multiple independent systems
- Result: Emergence within kilometers of target

*Rough Jump (minutes calculation):*

- Use approximate gravitational data
- Ignore minor sources
- Accept lower accuracy
- Overload cells to compensate for imprecision
- Result: Emergence within ~1000 km of target, destroyed jump cells

*Emergency Jump (seconds calculation):*

- Minimal gravitational data
- Massive overload of jump cells
- Random displacement likely
- Cell destruction guaranteed
- Result: Unknown destination, survive but lost

**Gravitational Interference:**

Jumping near massive objects (planets, stars, black holes) is dangerous:

- Gravity wells distort space unpredictably
- Small calculation errors magnified
- Risk of emergence inside solid matter
- Conservative doctrine: Jump from system edges, not close orbit

## Strategic Implications 
vs. Subluminal Travel:

- Subluminal drives: 30-50% light speed (weeks to months between systems)
- Jump drives: Hours to days (depending on calculation time)
- Jump drives revolutionize strategic mobility
- Subluminal remains standard for routine commerce (cheaper, safer)

**First Strike Capability:**

Jump drives enable surprise attacks:

- Appear without warning
- No approach time for defender preparation
- Strategic advantage in positioning forces

**Retreat Capability:**

Emergency jumps allow escape from defeat:

- Even losing battle can withdraw forces
- Makes complete victory difficult
- Encourages risk-taking (always have escape option)

**Logistics Challenge:**

Jump drives consume enormous energy:

- Frequent jumps deplete fuel reserves
- Need infrastructure for refueling
- Limits operational tempo
- Cannot jump continuously without resupply

## Confederation Limitations

**Incomplete Mastery:**

The Confederation only acquired jump technology 5 years ago:

- Still learning optimal procedures
- Jump calculations less reliable than Krell's
- Cell manufacturing more expensive
- Doctrine still developing

**Krell Advantage:**

Krell have decades of jump drive experience:

- Better calculations (faster and more accurate)
- More efficient cell designs
- Superior emergency jump procedures
- Institutional knowledge Confederation lacks

**Mysterious Origin:**

Neither Confederation nor Krell invented jump drives:

- Krell won't reveal where they got technology
- Could be: discovered artifact, stolen from precursors, independent invention
- Unknown if other civilizations possess similar capability
- Mystery suggests broader galactic history

## Safety Protocols

**Standard Procedures:**

- Never jump within 1 AU of star
- Never jump within 100,000 km of planet
- Always calculate from system edge
- Verify calculations through redundant systems
- Test jump cells before critical operations

**Emergency Procedures:**

- Only use when destruction imminent
- Accept cell destruction as inevitable
- Minimize calculation time but don't skip entirely
- Accept unknown destination as price of survival
- Immediately begin position verification after emergence

**Failure Modes:**

- Emergence inside solid matter: Instant destruction
- Emergence in star: Instant destruction
- Emergence in planet gravity well: Possible survival but dangerous
- Failed jump activation: Stranded, cells destroyed, no movement
- Partial jump: Theoretical—ship torn apart between dimensions

## Future Development

**Confederation Goals:**

- Match Krell calculation speed and accuracy
- Develop cheaper jump cell manufacturing
- Extend maximum reliable range
- Reduce jump energy requirements
- Better emergency jump protocols

**Theoretical Improvements:**

- Real-time gravitational sensing (reduce calculation time)
- Quantum entanglement navigation (perfect positioning)
- Multi-destination jump cells (reduce recalculation time)
- Miniaturization (personal jump devices? probably impossible)

**Connection to Other Technologies:**

The mathematical similarities between jump drives and phase-variance/warp corridors suggest:

- Unified spacetime manipulation principles
- Possible hybrid technologies
- Unknown applications waiting to be discovered
- Common origin or convergent physics?


# Visible Light Communication (VLC)

## Core Principle

Since radio waves cannot penetrate phase-variance boundaries, phased personnel communicate via modulated visible light. This evolved from crude flashlight Morse code to sophisticated military-grade optical communication systems.

## Technical Evolution

**Act I (Crude Improvisation):**

- Sergeant Rodriguez uses tactical flashlight for Morse code
- Phased legionaries can see each other's lights
- Limited bandwidth (~10-20 words per minute)
- No standardization or formal protocols
- Proof of concept only

**Act II (Standardized Protocols):**

- Development of standardized tactical light signals
- Helmet-mounted LED emitters for hands-free communication
- Pre-programmed message sequences
- Basic relay protocols
- Bandwidth: ~50-100 words per minute equivalent

**Act III (Military-Grade Systems):**

- Sophisticated pulse-modulation encoding
- Automatic relay drones
- Multi-node network capability
- Integration with tactical displays
- Bandwidth: ~500+ words per minute equivalent

## Technical Specifications

**Hardware:**

Helmet-Mounted Emitters:

- High-intensity LED arrays
- 360-degree emission pattern (visible to nearby phased personnel)
- Multiple wavelengths for redundancy
- Power draw: 1-2W continuous
- Battery life: 10+ hours

*Relay Nodes:*

- Small transceivers (5cm cube)
- Placed while phased, become visible when released
- Omnidirectional reception and transmission
- Self-powered (12-hour battery)
- Vulnerable once visible

*Integration:*

- Linked to tactical computers
- Can display received text on helmet HUD
- Can transmit from pre-programmed message library
- Manual override for custom messages

**Communication Protocols:**

*Standard Tactical Signals:*

- Unit status reports
- Movement commands
- Target designation
- Medical emergency
- Ammunition status
- Phase shield status
- Rally point designation

*Message Priority System:*

- Emergency (flashing red): Immediate threat, medical emergency
- Urgent (rapid yellow): Time-sensitive tactical information
- Routine (steady blue): Status updates, acknowledgments
- Query (pulsing green): Requesting information

**Network Topology:**

- Point-to-point: Direct soldier-to-soldier
- Mesh network: Multiple soldiers maintaining network
- Relay extension: Drones extend range around corners
- Hub system: Designated soldier coordinates communications

## Critical Limitations

**1. Line of Sight Absolute:**

- Light cannot pass through walls, bulkheads, obstacles
- Corners break communication
- Smoke, debris, or steam obscures signals
- No bend-around capabilities like radio

**2. Range Limited:**

- Effective range: ~50 meters in clear conditions
- Degraded by distance, angles, environmental conditions
- Relay nodes required for extended operations
- Large ships require multiple relay chains

**3. Bandwidth Constraints:**

- Much slower than radio communication
- Complex information difficult to transmit
- No video or high-resolution data
- Primarily text-based tactical messages

**4. Network Fragility:**

Once combat starts, VLC networks degrade rapidly:

- Relay nodes destroyed by fire
- Smoke/debris blocks signals
- Moving personnel break line-of-sight
- Network coverage collapses in minutes

**5. Detection Risk:**

- Relay nodes visible once placed
- Visible light could theoretically be detected by enemy sensors
- Phased personnel's emitters invisible to normal-phase observers
- But relays are vulnerabilities

## Tactical Applications

**Pre-Materialization Coordination:**

While phased, teams use VLC to:

- Report positioning
- Coordinate staggered materialization timing
- Identify targets
- Share reconnaissance data
- Synchronize actions

**During Combat:**

Once fighting starts, VLC degrades but remains useful for:

- Short-range coordination (within sight)
- Fallback rally points
- Medical emergency signals
- Ammunition sharing

**Relay Deployment:**

- Placed during phase-in to create network
- Enables coordination across ship sections
- Vulnerable once combat starts
- Must be considered expendable

## Training Requirements

**Basic VLC Proficiency:**

- All boarding specialists must be fluent in standard signals
- ~40 hours training to proficiency
- Regular drill to maintain speed
- Integration with other tactical training

**Advanced Network Operations:**

- Relay deployment tactics
- Network troubleshooting
- Improvisation when network fails
- Alternative signaling methods

**Contingency Communication:**

Teams train for VLC failure:

- Pre-mission coordination sufficient for initial actions
- Fall back to independent operation per mission command doctrine
- Physical hand signals for close-range coordination
- Accept that complex coordination becomes impossible

# Future Development

**Potential Improvements:**

- Longer range emitters
- Better beam focusing (reduce detection)
- Faster modulation (higher bandwidth)
- Automatic relay deployment drones
- Integration with micro-drone swarms
- Quantum entanglement communication (theoretical—would work while phased?)

**Current Research:**

- Trying to develop communication that penetrates phase boundary
- No success yet—physics seems to prohibit it
- VLC remains only option for phased coordination


# Drone Systems

## Micro-Drones (2cm Class)

**Specifications:**

- Size: ~2 centimeters (insect-sized)
- Weight: 10-15 grams
- Propulsion: Miniaturized electric fans
- Sensors: Visual (basic camera), thermal, motion detection
- Power: 30-minute flight time
- Communication: Short-range optical to nearby drones
- Loadout: 30 per soldier (special harness)
- Cost: ~500 credits each (expendable)

**Capabilities:**

- Autonomous operation (no continuous control needed)
- Form teams of 6 for extended sensing
- Scout ahead around corners
- Alert soldiers to threats via optical signal
- Can double as VLC relay nodes
- Fast and maneuverable in confined spaces

**Tactical Use:**

- Deploy during phase-in or after materialization
- Create sensor network throughout ship sections
- Provide early warning of enemy movement
- Identify ambush points and hidden threats
- Expendable—expect high attrition

**Survival:**

- 85% survive initial insertion
- 40% still operational by mission end
- Lost to: enemy fire, collision, power exhaustion, electronic warfare
- Small size and speed primary defense

**Limitations:**

- Limited sensor capability (basic visual/thermal)
- Short flight time (30 minutes)
- Vulnerable to EMP/electronic warfare
- Cannot carry weapons or equipment
- Minimal processing power

## Standard Combat Drones (20-30cm Class)

**Specifications:**

- Size: 20-30 centimeters
- Weight: 2-5 kg
- Propulsion: More powerful fans/thrusters
- Sensors: Advanced visual, thermal, electromagnetic, audio
- Power: 90-minute flight time
- Weapons: Integrated explosives (500g equivalent) or small firearms
- Loadout: 1 per soldier or specialized backpack/armor mount
- Cost: ~50,000 credits each (significant investment)

**Capabilities:**

- Combat engagement (direct attack capability)
- Heavy relay deployment (better range and power)
- Advanced reconnaissance
- Target designation
- Can deploy micro-drones as cargo
- Controlled via tactical computer or autonomous

**Tactical Use:**

- Deploy during or after insertion
- Engage enemy directly with onboard weapons
- Deploy relay networks for VLC
- Scout dangerous areas before troops enter
- Suppress enemy during critical moments

**Combat Modes:**

- Kamikaze: Fly directly at target and detonate (one-use)
- Harassment: Strafe enemy positions with weapons
- Recon: Extended surveillance of areas
- Relay: Deploy and maintain VLC network nodes

**Limitations:**

- Not shielded (detectable by enemy sensors)
- Limited ammunition/explosives
- More expensive—not truly expendable
- Larger size makes easier target
- Require more control attention than micro-drones

## Deployment Doctrine

**Phase-In Protocol:**

- Soldiers phase in carrying drones (all phased together)
- Once positioned, soldiers remain phased
- Drones released (break contact, become visible)
- Drones deploy rapidly to scout/setup relays
- Risk: Drones visible, could alert enemy

**Post-Materialization Protocol:**

- Soldiers materialize and establish perimeter
- Deploy drones immediately for expanded sensing
- Use drones to extend tactical awareness beyond line of sight
- More common approach—lower risk of compromise

**Trade-offs:**

- Speed (phase-in deployment) vs. Stealth (post-materialization deployment)
- Enemy may detect drones before soldiers materialize
- But drones provide critical intelligence for positioning
- Tactical decision based on mission priorities

## Integration with VLC

**Synergy:**

- Micro-drones can serve as VLC relay nodes
- Standard drones deploy dedicated relays
- Creates layered communication network
- Extends VLC range beyond line-of-sight limits

**Network Architecture:**

- Micro-drones: Short-range mesh network
- Standard drones: Long-range backbone relays
- Soldier emitters: Local coordination
- Integrated system provides coverage until combat degrades it

## Manufacturing and Supply

**Production:**

- Micro-drones: Mass-produced, 1000s per day across Confederation
- Standard drones: Specialized manufacturing, 100s per week
- Both require sophisticated facilities but not exotic materials

**Logistics:**

- Soldiers carry micro-drones as standard loadout (30 each)
- Standard drones allocated based on mission requirements
- Replacements shipped regularly to units
- Not supply-constrained like phase-shields or anchors

## Future Development

**Planned Improvements:**

- Longer flight times (better batteries)
- Quieter propulsion (stealth)
- Better AI (more autonomous operation)
- Improved sensors (detect phased objects?)
- Modular payloads (swap weapons/sensors/relays)

**Experimental Concepts:**

- Nano-drones (millimeter scale)
- Shielded drones (can phase with soldiers)
- Swarm coordination (hundreds operating as single unit)
- AI-directed tactical suggestions


# Standard Combat Systems

## Kinetic Impellers

**Principle:**

Magnetic acceleration launches projectiles at 15% light speed using sequential electromagnetic coils.
Specifications:

- Projectile velocity: ~45,000 km/s
- Range: Effectively unlimited in space
- Projectile size: 1kg to 1000kg depending on weapon
- Firing rate: 1-10 rounds per minute (ship-scale)
- Accuracy: Extremely high at short range, requires prediction at long range

**Advantages:**

- Silent (no exhaust signature until impact)
- Invisible (projectile too small/fast to see)
- Devastating kinetic energy (relativistic effects at 15% c)
- Simple ammunition (no explosives needed)
- Difficult to counter (no exhaust to track)

**Disadvantages:**

- Light-speed delay (target may move before impact at long range)
- Requires targeting prediction
- Limited ammunition capacity
- Recoil requires stabilization

**Confederation Primary Weapon:**

The Confederation favors kinetics for their reliability and effectiveness. Most capital ships mount multiple kinetic impeller batteries as primary armament.


## Plasma Lances

**Principle:**

Superheated plasma contained in magnetic bottles, fired at near-light speed. Preferred by Krell.

**Specifications:**

- Plasma velocity: ~90% light speed
- Temperature: 10+ million Kelvin
- Range: 10,000-50,000 km (magnetic containment degrades)
- Firing rate: 5-20 rounds per minute
- Energy per shot: 100+ GJ

**Advantages:**

- Extremely high damage at close range
- Punches through most armor
- Explosive effect on impact
- Visual intimidation factor

**Disadvantages:**

- Short range (containment fails)
- High energy consumption
- Visible launch (plasma glow)
- Heat signature detectable

**Krell Primary Weapon:**

Krell favor plasma lances for close-range overwhelming firepower. Matches their aggressive tactical doctrine.

## Gravitic Mines

**Principle:**

Create localized intense gravity wells that crush any ship entering the field.

**Specifications:**

- Field radius: 100-500 meters
- Gravity intensity: 1000+ g at center
- Duration: Hours (powered) or minutes (self-powered)
- Deployment: From ships or as static defenses

**Advantages:**

- Area denial (ships cannot pass through)
- No ammunition—gravitational effect
- Difficult to detect until activated
- Effective against all ship types

**Disadvantages:**

- Static position (easily avoided once detected)
- Limited duration if self-powered
- Complex technology (expensive)
- Friendly fire risk if mispositioned

**Tactical Use:**

- Deny maneuver space during battle
- Protect retreat routes
- Channel enemy into kill zones
- Disrupt enemy jump calculations

## Disruptor Missiles

**Principle:**

Long-range missiles carrying electromagnetic pulse generators designed to disable electronics and sensors.

**Specifications:**

- Range: 100,000+ km
- Velocity: 0.3c
- EMP radius: 10-50 km
- Effect: Disable sensors, communications, some weapons
- Duration: Temporary (seconds to minutes)

**Advantages:**

- Disable before destroy (reduces enemy capability)
- Long range engagement
- Non-lethal option available
- Creates tactical opportunities

**Disadvantages:**

- Doesn't directly damage ships
- Enemy can recover
- Countermeasures possible (Faraday shielding)
- Expensive per shot

**Tactical Use:**

- "Blind" enemy before kinetic strikes
- Disrupt enemy coordination
- Create windows for boarding actions
- Force enemy to close distance

## Nano-Swarms

**Principle:**

Clouds of microscopic machines that attach to enemy hulls and systematically dismantle armor, weapons, and sensors.

**Specifications:**

- Swarm size: Billions of nanomachines
- Deployment range: 1,000-5,000 km
- Effect time: Minutes to dismantle armor
- Power: Wireless from launching ship
- Control: Autonomous after release

**Advantages:**

- Terrifying psychological effect
- Bypasses shields and armor
- Difficult to counter directly
- Progressive damage over time

**Disadvantages:**
*1. Power Dependency:*

- Swarms draw power from ship under attack
- If ship's power fails completely, swarms decay in minutes
- Target can power down own ship to neutralize swarms

*2. Countermeasures:*

- Point-defense systems can destroy swarms in flight
- ECM can disrupt swarm coordination
- Target can shut down own power (risky—leaves defenseless)
- Evasive maneuvers can prevent attachment

*3. Manufacturing Complexity:*

- Incredibly expensive and difficult to manufacture
- Limited supplies across entire fleets
- Used sparingly in decisive engagements only
- Cannot be easily replaced

*4. Attachment Difficulty:*

- Swarms must physically reach and attach to hull
- Distance reduces effectiveness
- Ship maneuvering can shake off swarms
- Not instant effect—takes time

**Tactical Use:**

- Decisive engagements only (too expensive for routine use)
- Target critical ships (flagships, carriers)
- Force enemy to choose: defend or attack
- Psychological weapon (forces defensive posture)

- Fundamental forces cannot be ignored

- Manufacturing complexity limits production
- Some technologies remain strategic assets

**Practical:**

- Logistics remain fundamental constraint
- Doctrine must match capabilities
