# The Stellar Republic - Government

## Document Summary

This document details the federal government structure of the Stellar Republic, adapted from Roman principles with English-evolved terminology developed over 600 years of colony evolution.

**Federal Charter (Constitution) - Key Principles:**
1. **Subsidiarity**: Federal government limited to explicit powers; all else reserved to systems
2. **Balanced Magistrates**: Multiple executives checking each other
3. **Deliberative Supremacy**: Senate controls policy; executives execute
4. **Merit-Based Service**: Leadership earned through demonstrated competence
5. **Rule of Law**: Written constitution binding on all, including leaders
6. **Individual Rights**: Protections for all residents, enhanced for citizens

**Federal Executive Branch:**
- **Dual Consuls** (2): 5-year staggered terms, command military, execute laws, foreign relations, mutual veto power
- **Federal Praetors** (12): 8-year terms, interpret Charter, resolve system disputes, judicial review
- **Federal Quaestors** (6): 4-year terms, manage treasury, execute budget, audit systems
- **Federal Censors** (2): 2-year terms every 10 years, conduct census, review Senate, maintain civic standards

**Federal Legislative Branch:**
- **Federal Senate**: 126 members (3 per system), 15-year terms, declare war (2/3), ratify treaties (2/3), budget (majority)
- **12 Standing Committees**: Military Affairs, Foreign Relations, Finance, Infrastructure, Science, Justice, Education, Trade, Immigration, Health, Intelligence, Constitutional Affairs

**Federal Departments** (~80,000 civilian employees total):
- Defense, Foreign Affairs, Infrastructure, Trade & Commerce, Finance, Justice, Immigration & Citizenship, Education, Health & Medicine, Scientific Research

**System-Level Government:**
- System Senate (50-200 members, 10-year terms)
- System Consuls (2, 3-year terms)
- System Praetors, Quaestors
- Control: System budget, militia, infrastructure, justice, education

**Checks and Balances:**
- Federal vs System powers clearly delineated
- Senate controls budget, Consuls execute, mutual veto
- Praetors independent with judicial review
- Popular sovereignty through petitions and referendums

**Political Culture:** Consensus-seeking, transparent, accountable, professional, civic duty emphasis

## PART VII: FEDERAL GOVERNMENT (ENGLISH NOMENCLATURE)

The Republic's government structure adapts Roman principles with English-evolved terminology that developed over 600 years of isolated colony evolution.

### The Federal Charter (Constitution)

**Official Name**: "The Charter of Federal Unity" (commonly "Federal Charter" or just "the Charter")

**Key Principles**:
1. **Subsidiarity**: Federal government limited to explicit powers; all else reserved to systems
2. **Balanced Magistrates**: Multiple executives checking each other
3. **Deliberative Supremacy**: Senate controls policy; executives execute
4. **Merit-Based Service**: Leadership earned through demonstrated competence
5. **Rule of Law**: Written constitution binding on all, including leaders
6. **Individual Rights**: Protections for all residents, enhanced for citizens

### Federal Executive Branch

**DUAL CONSULS** ("First Consuls" or "Federal Consuls"):

**Selection**: Elected by Federal Senate for 5-year terms, staggered (one elected every 2.5 years)

**Requirements**:
- Full Citizen for minimum 15 years
- Minimum age 50
- Completed federal service (cursus honorum)
- Cannot serve more than two terms (10 years total)
- One must have military background, one civilian (by tradition, not law)

**Powers**:
- **Command federal military**: Supreme commanders of Stellar Legions
- **Execute federal law**: Administer federal agencies and departments
- **Foreign relations**: Negotiate treaties (Senate must ratify)
- **Emergency authority**: Can act decisively in crisis (Senate must confirm within 6 months)
- **Veto**: Each Consul can veto other Consul's actions (deadlock resolved by Senate vote)

**Current Consuls (2750)**:
- **First Consul Marcus Varro**: Former Legion Legate, military background, age 58, year 3 of first term
- **Second Consul Elena Torres**: Former Chief Administrator, civilian background, age 62, year 1 of second term

**Staff**:
- **Federal Council**: Cabinet of department heads advising Consuls
- **Office of Consuls**: Administrative staff supporting executive functions
- **Security Council**: Military and intelligence advisors

---

**FEDERAL PRAETORS** (Supreme Judicial Officers):

**Number**: 12 (enough for complex cases to be heard by panels while allowing courts to function continuously)

**Selection**: Elected by Federal Senate for 8-year terms, cannot be removed except for misconduct

**Requirements**:
- Full Citizen for minimum 20 years
- Minimum age 45
- Legal experience (minimum 15 years practice or judicial service)
- Must have served in Civic Administration (judicial branch)

**Powers**:
- **Interpret Federal Charter**: Final authority on constitutional questions
- **Resolve system disputes**: Arbitrate conflicts between member systems
- **Federal criminal cases**: Ultimate appeals for federal crimes
- **Judicial review**: Can invalidate federal laws violating Charter

**Organization**:
- **Chief Praetor**: Elected by fellow Praetors for 2-year term, first among equals
- **Panels**: Cases heard by 3-5 Praetors (varies by importance)
- **Full Court**: All 12 Praetors for constitutional cases
- **Regional Circuit**: Praetors travel to different systems hearing cases (ensuring access)

**Current Chief Praetor (2750)**: Sarah Kim, age 54, former system judge from Nova Roma, year 6 of term

---

**FEDERAL QUAESTORS** (Financial Officers):

**Number**: 6

**Selection**: Elected by Federal Senate for 4-year terms

**Requirements**:
- Full Citizen for minimum 10 years
- Minimum age 35
- Financial or economic expertise
- Completed Civic Administration service in finance

**Responsibilities**:
- **Manage federal treasury**: Collect revenues, pay expenditures
- **Budget execution**: Ensure funds spent as Senate authorized
- **Audit systems**: Verify member systems meet contribution obligations
- **Financial reporting**: Quarterly reports to Senate on fiscal status
- **Debt management**: Issue federal bonds, manage long-term obligations

**Organization**:
- **Chief Quaestor**: Coordinates other five, appointed by Consuls from the six
- **Specialized portfolios**: Military finance, infrastructure, debt, revenue, audit, systems

**Current Chief Quaestor (2750)**: David Okonkwo, age 47, former system treasurer from Builder's Legacy

---

**FEDERAL CENSORS** (Standards and Census Officers):

**Number**: 2

**Selection**: Elected by Federal Senate for 2-year terms every 10 years (only serve briefly, then return to private life)

**Requirements**:
- Full Citizen for minimum 25 years
- Minimum age 55
- Distinguished career in public service
- Cannot have held office in past 5 years (ensures independence)

**Responsibilities**:
- **Conduct federal census**: Comprehensive population count every 10 years
- **Review Senate membership**: Assess whether senators still meet standards
- **Maintain civic standards**: Publicly censure officials for ethical violations (not criminal, but moral)
- **Constitutional fidelity**: Ensure federal government adheres to Charter principles

**Powers**:
- **Censure**: Public reprimand for misconduct (damages reputation but not criminal)
- **Senate review**: Recommend removing senators for failure to meet obligations
- **Census authority**: Compel cooperation with population count
- **Public trust**: Investigating corruption allegations

**Note**: Censors are temporary position, activated only during census years (every 10 years). Position created to prevent permanent oversight body from accumulating too much power.

**Current Censors** (elected 2750 for 2750-2752 census period):
- Julia Martinez, age 59, retired Legion Legate
- Ahmed Hassan, age 63, retired system senator from Eternal Vigil

### Federal Legislative Branch

**FEDERAL SENATE**:

**Size**: 126 members (3 per member system Ã— 42 systems)

**Selection**: Appointed by system senates from those who completed federal cursus honorum

**Terms**: 15 years (long term accounts for communication delays and provides continuity)

**Requirements**:
- Full Citizen for minimum 15 years
- Minimum age 45
- Completed service in multiple systems (rotational requirement)
- Passed all citizenship examinations with exemplary scores

**Powers**:
- **Declare war**: Requires 2/3 majority
- **Ratify treaties**: Requires 2/3 majority
- **Approve federal budget**: Simple majority
- **Enact federal laws**: Simple majority (but limited to federal jurisdiction)
- **Elect federal officers**: Consuls, Praetors, Quaestors, Censors
- **Confirm appointments**: Senior military commanders, department heads
- **Conduct investigations**: Oversight of federal agencies
- **Impeach**: Can remove federal officers for misconduct (2/3 majority)

**Organization**:
- **President of Senate**: Elected by senators for 3-year term, presides over sessions
- **Committees**: 12 standing committees covering different policy areas
  - Military Affairs
  - Foreign Relations
  - Finance and Budget
  - Infrastructure and Engineering
  - Science and Technology
  - Justice and Legal Affairs
  - Education and Culture
  - Trade and Commerce
  - Immigration and Citizenship
  - Health and Medical
  - Intelligence and Security
  - Constitutional Affairs

**Sessions**:
- **Regular session**: Convenes annually for 4 months at capital
- **Emergency session**: Can be called by Consuls or petition of 20% of senators
- **Virtual attendance**: Senators can participate remotely due to distances (but physical presence preferred for important votes)

**Current Senate President (2750)**: Marcus Chen, age 58, senator from Founder's Oath, year 2 of presidency

**LEGISLATIVE PROCESS**:

1. **Proposal**: Senator introduces bill in committee
2. **Committee Review**: Committee debates, amends, votes
3. **Floor Debate**: If committee approves, full Senate debates (typically 2-4 weeks)
4. **Senate Vote**: Simple majority passes (except treaties and war declarations requiring 2/3)
5. **Consul Review**: Consuls can veto (rarely used; political cost high)
6. **Override**: Senate can override Consul veto with 2/3 majority
7. **Judicial Review**: Praetors can review if constitutional challenge filed

**Deliberate Speed**: Process designed to be thoughtful, not fast. Average bill takes 6-12 months from proposal to enactment. Emergency measures can move faster (weeks) but require supermajority.

### Federal Departments (Civilian Bureaucracy)

**Department of Defense**:
- Civilian oversight of military
- Budget and logistics
- Does not command (Consuls command; department supplies)
- **Secretary**: Civilian Full Citizen appointed by Consuls, confirmed by Senate
- Staff: 5,000 personnel

**Department of Foreign Affairs**:
- Diplomacy with other factions
- Treaty negotiation
- Intelligence coordination (civilian intelligence, not military)
- **Secretary**: Career diplomat appointed by Consuls
- Staff: 2,000 personnel plus diplomatic missions

**Department of Infrastructure**:
- Coordinates federal construction projects
- Works with Engineering Corps
- Allocates resources to systems
- **Secretary**: Usually former Engineering Corps
- Staff: 10,000 personnel (engineers, planners, project managers)

**Department of Trade and Commerce**:
- Regulates inter-system trade
- Prevents monopolies
- Ensures fair practices
- **Secretary**: Economist or business leader
- Staff: 3,000 personnel (regulators, economists, trade specialists)

**Department of Finance**:
- Collects federal taxes
- Manages revenue
- Works with Quaestors
- **Secretary**: Financial expert
- Staff: 8,000 personnel (tax collectors, auditors, accountants)

**Department of Justice**:
- Federal law enforcement
- Prosecution of federal crimes
- Federal public defenders
- **Attorney General**: Senior lawyer appointed by Consuls
- Staff: 4,000 personnel (prosecutors, investigators, defenders)

**Department of Immigration and Citizenship**:
- Process citizenship applications
- Manage refugee resettlement
- Maintain citizenship registry
- **Secretary**: Usually former Civic Administration
- Staff: 6,000 personnel (case workers, investigators, administrators)

**Department of Education**:
- Oversees Civic Academies
- Manages Federal Academy
- Sets educational standards
- **Secretary**: Educator or former professor
- Staff: 15,000 personnel (teachers, administrators, curriculum developers)

**Department of Health and Medicine**:
- Coordinates federal healthcare
- Works with Medical Service
- Disease surveillance and prevention
- **Surgeon General**: Senior physician from Medical Service
- Staff: 20,000 personnel (public health, medical administration, research)

**Department of Scientific Research**:
- Funds research institutes
- Coordinates with Scientific Expedition
- Manages technology sharing
- **Chief Scientist**: Prominent researcher
- Staff: 5,000 personnel (administrators, research coordinators)

**Total Federal Bureaucracy**: ~80,000 civilian employees (for population of 200-300 billion, remarkably lean government)

### System-Level Government

**System Senate**:
- 50-200 members (varies by population)
- Elected from Full Citizens who completed system service
- Terms: 10 years
- Powers: Control system budget, militia, infrastructure, justice, education

**System Consuls**:
- 2 executives elected by system senate
- Terms: 3 years
- Powers: Execute system laws, command system militia, represent system federally

**System Praetors**:
- Judges for system-level cases
- Elected by system senate
- Terms: 6 years

**System Quaestors**:
- Financial officers
- Manage system budgets
- Terms: 4 years

### Checks and Balances

**Federal vs. System**:
- Federal: Defense, foreign relations, inter-system commerce, currency
- System: Internal governance, education, infrastructure, justice, militia
- Overlap: Regulated by Federal Charter and adjudicated by Praetors

**Executive vs. Legislative**:
- Senate controls budget (executives cannot spend without authorization)
- Consuls can veto (Senate can override 2/3)
- Senate confirms appointments
- Consuls execute but Senate investigates

**Judicial Independence**:
- Praetors elected for fixed terms, cannot be removed
- Interpret Charter independent of political pressure
- Can invalidate laws (rare but powerful)

**Popular Sovereignty**:
- Citizens can petition Senate (10,000 signatures forces committee hearing)
- System referendums can override system senates
- Federal constitutional amendments require ratification by 3/4 of systems' popular votes

### Political Culture

**Consensus-Seeking**:
- Senate operates by deliberation and persuasion
- Majority rule but minority rights respected
- Compromise valued over ideological purity

**Transparency**:
- All Senate sessions public (except classified security briefings)
- Budget publicly available
- Officials file financial disclosures

**Accountability**:
- Annual reports from all magistrates
- Regular audits by Quaestors
- Public can attend most government meetings

**Professionalism**:
- Officials expected to be competent, not just popular
- Technical expertise valued
- Long-term thinking over short-term gains

**Civic Duty**:
- Holding office seen as service, not personal enrichment
- Corruption scandals rare but devastating to careers
- Former officials often return to private life rather than becoming professional politicians
