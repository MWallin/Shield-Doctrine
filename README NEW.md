## Project Overview

**Shield Doctrine** is a military science fiction trilogy following the development of revolutionary phase-variance shield technology and the Shield Doctrine boarding tactics in a Roman-inspired stellar republic. The story emphasizes professional competence over heroics, systematic innovation over genius breakthroughs, and earned citizenship through service.

**Current Status:** Act I v8 (aligned with Stellar Republic worldbuilding), Acts II-III v5

## Core Themes (Story Foundation)

1. **Professional Competence Overcomes Adversity** - Success through skill and training, not luck
2. **Earned Citizenship Creates Stakeholder Society** - Service grants political voice
3. **Infrastructure as Foundation of Civilization** - Roads, stations, systems matter
4. **Innovation Within Institutional Frameworks** - No maverick rebels, systematic improvement
5. **Mission Command: Decentralized Execution** - Subordinates execute with initiative
6. **Primary Group Cohesion** - Fight for comrades, not abstractions
7. **Technology as Tool, Not Solution** - Tech enables, people accomplish
8. **Mercy to Defeated Enemies (Clementia)** - Strategic and moral virtue
9. **Sacrifice in Service of Worthy Causes** - Costs honored but not glorified
10. **Mystery of Deeper Origins** - Ancient civilizations, unanswered questions

See **correctness/Story Themes Guide.md** for detailed explanations.

## Worldbuilding Pillars

### **The Stellar Republic (2750 CE)**

- **Government:** Federal republic with Senate, dual Consuls, earned citizenship
- **Military:** Professional volunteer Stellar Legions (Roman-inspired)
- **Service Branches:** 6 paths to citizenship (Engineering, Medical, Scientific, Civic Admin, Legions, Vigiles)
- **Citizenship:** 4 tiers (Non-Citizen â†’ Junior Resident â†’ Resident â†’ Full Citizen)
- **Population:** 200-300 billion across 42 star systems
- **Technology Level:** Jump drives (recent), no FTL communication, phase-shields (new discovery)

### **Technology Constraints**

- **Jump Drives:** Spatial compression FTL, exponential energy cost, calculation vs. accuracy tradeoff
- **Phase-Shields:** Recently discovered, shift out of phase, no communication while phased
- **No FTL Comms:** Physical distance = communication delay
- **Hard Physics:** Consistent rules, no magic solutions

See **world-building/** folder for complete documentation.

## Story Synopsis (Trilogy)

### **Act I: Obsidian Station (Year 2750, Day 0)**
- Dr. Adeyemi discovers phase-shields from mysterious artifact
- Firestorm Mercenaries attack station (Corporate Syndicate wants artifact)
- Centurion Chen's legionaries deploy shields in desperate combat
- Commander Varro makes emergency jump, convoy stranded 4 days
- Investigation reveals Webb's betrayal, broader artifact trafficking network
- Introduction to Shield Doctrine possibilities

### **Act II: Typhon (6 weeks later)**
- Systematic Shield Doctrine development and training
- Torres-Adeyemi mathematical breakthrough (shields/jump drives linked)
- Tanaka's investigation continues, citizenship vulnerability revealed
- Battle of Typhon: First planned use of Shield Doctrine
- VLC (Very Low Confidence) jump + boarding tactics succeed
- Validation and expansion authorized

### **Act III: Hammerfall (16 months later)**
- Shield Doctrine scaled up, multiple legions trained
- Ambitious multi-fleet operation
- Phase-anchor technology (experimental, 75% success rate)
- Technology fails, professional adaptation succeeds anyway
- Captain Park's sacrifice, 136 legionaries KIA
- Victory validates doctrine, citizenship reforms implemented
- Epilogue: Artifact mysteries deepen, future threats hinted

## Writing Guidelines

### **Tone: Noblebright**
- Optimistic but realistic
- Competent people solving hard problems
- Institutions can be good (while imperfect)
- Sacrifice meaningful for worthy causes
- Technology serves people, not vice versa

See **correctness/Noblebright Tone Guide.md**

### **Style: Hard Military SF**
- Technical accuracy matters
- Show systematic problem-solving
- No deus ex machina solutions
- Respect intelligence of characters and readers
- Combined arms integration

See **correctness/Narrative Conventions Guide.md**

### **Roman Integration**
- Military: Centuries, centurions, gladius, systematic training
- Civic: Earned citizenship, Senate, infrastructure priority
- Cultural: Service ethos, professional development, clementia

See **correctness/Roman Principles Integration Guide.md**
