## PART I: THE NEW SUBPLOT FRAMEWORK

### The Galactic Context

**The Artifacts**:
- Ancient encyclopedia created millions of years ago by precursor civilization (now extinct)
- Designed to bootstrap developing species
- Written in nearly undecipherable language using impossible concepts
- Housed in uncrackable shielding
- Intended for species "smart enough to read it, smart enough to use it"
- Scattered across this region of galaxy
- Most found artifacts discarded as worthless rocks

**The "Partners" (Naive Aliens)**:
- Unknown species name (Corporate Syndicate calls them "Partners" = business partners)
- First contact: Corps exploration mission found Partner outpost
- Communication extremely difficult (different frames of reference; only math/science works)
- Technology similar to humans except **advanced spaceflight**
- Found method to open artifacts (can't decode contents but studying internals bootstrapped their tech)
- Culturally cannot conceive of deception/lying
- Searching for artifacts to recover "lost" precursor knowledge

**The Corporate Syndicate ("Corps")**:
- 32-star-system faction from Stellar Republic worldbuilding
- Corporate-funded colony ships, governance by shareholder vote
- Efficient, wealthy, extreme inequality
- Views Republic with suspicion (civic virtue = authoritarianism)
- **Encountered Partners ~10-20 years ago**
- Realized Partners can't detect lies; exploited them ruthlessly
- Traded one artifact (museum rock) for advanced ship
- Now reverse-engineering Partner ship technology
- **Artifact hunting network**: Systematic search across known space

**Corps Artifact Operations**:
- Developed scanner for artifact radiation signature
- Network of informants across multiple factions targeting research institutions
- Informants report discoveries to handlers
- Handlers decide approach: bribery, discrete murder, mercenary raid
- Key principle: No direct Corps involvement, no traces
- Uses freelance mercenaries (Firestorm and others)

### Webb's Story

**Background** (not all shown in Act I):
- Academic track student with gambling problem (years ago)
- Wanted to fit in with wealthy peers, spent beyond means
- "Friend" (handler) helped with debts, proposed "friendly deal"
- Pattern established: Webb provides research, handler pays debts
- Became addicted to lifestyle
- Handler introduced artifact scheme: huge payouts for authentic artifact intel

**Handler "Aurelius"**:
- Corporate Syndicate talent scout
- Professional industrial espionage operative
- Poses as Republic insider (frontier settlement looking for edge)
- Years-long relationship with Webb
- Webb doesn't know he works for Corps (thinks Republic underdog corporation)
- Recent: Instructed Webb to watch for xenoarchaeological artifacts
- "Special collectors willing to pay good money"

**Webb's Psychology**:
- Views himself as **in control**, not victim
- "This was my idea, I can stop anytime"
- Ego prevents seeing manipulation
- Rationalizes greed: "I'm helping Republic underdogs get ahead"
- Thinks handler offers potential position if things work out
- This delusion makes interrogation breakdown devastating

**Webb's Role at Obsidian**:
- Communications crew (not researcher)
- Administrative access to station equipment
- Responsible for ensuring communication systems work
- Perfect position for data smuggling

### Miller's Story

**Background**:
- Member of Dr. Adeyemi's research team
- Legitimate access to his work
- Webb has leverage over her (blackmail, kept vague)
- Tricked/coerced into helping Webb initially
- Helps in hope Webb will honor promise to destroy evidence

**The Fatal Meeting** (6 hours before attack):
- Miller provides artifact data + shield research on drive
- Verbal confirmation: artifact radiation profile matches Webb's target type
- Webb excited, lets slip: "They'd have paid twice as much if I'd mentioned the shields with the coordinates!"
- Miller freezes, processes
- Miller: "You sent our location?! To whom?!"
- Webb stammers, Miller shoves him for response
- Fight escalates, Webb shoves hard
- Miller falls, hits head on communication terminal
- Dies instantly
- Webb hears legionnaires approaching, flees
- Rodriguez/Park find body minutes later

### The Attack

**Handler's Decision**:
- Receives Webb's artifact confirmation
- Realizes Republic won't sell artifact
- Tired of Webb's manipulation attempts
- Solution: Contract Firestorm Mercs to retrieve artifact + kill Webb
- "Solve all problems in one move"
- Underestimates Webb's cowardice and legionnaire stubbornness

**Firestorm Mercenaries**:
- Legitimate independent mercenary company (like Wagner Group)
- Main base unknown to Republic
- Outposts in many factions, easy to contact with resources
- Disregard for civilian life (shunned by Republic but not banned)
- Operate in Republic but "kind of behave" (respect legionnaire watching)
- Reputation: "Expensive, ruthless, get job done whatever it takes"
- "War crimes just cost a bit more"
- Emblem: Black circle with dancing flames from skull
- Full-face masks with logo (no individual faces visible)
- Professional soldiers using intimidation tactics

**Death-on-Capture Protocol**:
- All mercs have neck explosives
- Timer starts before assault, reset during, deactivated after
- Makes interrogation nearly impossible
- Creates willingness to take devastating losses
- Operational security: boots on ground know nothing useful

**The Attack Plan**:
- 3-4 weeks between Webb's artifact report and attack (travel time)
- 200 merc assault troops + 13 heavy assault suits
- Objective: Secure artifact, kill everyone, no witnesses
- Underestimated legionnaire defensive capabilities

### Tanaka's Investigation

**What She Discovers in Act I**:
- Miller murdered 6 hours before attack
- Webb's data smuggling program (piggybacks on outgoing transmissions)
- Station coordinates sent 3-4 weeks ago
- Artifact was target (from Webb's confession)
- Handler "Aurelius" exists, recruited Webb years ago
- Large trafficking network ("much bigger than Webb")
- Firestorm Mercs hired by well-funded unknown party

**What She Doesn't Know (Act I End)**:
- Handler's true identity
- Corps involvement
- Partners' existence
- Full scope of artifact network
- Why artifacts are valuable

**What She Suspects**:
- Handler has significant resources (hired Firestorm)
- Multiple informants likely across Republic
- Artifact trafficking is systematic, not opportunistic
- Webb was disposable (mercs sent to kill him)
