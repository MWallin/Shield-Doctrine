# Stellar Republic vs Story v5: Major Inconsistencies

**Purpose**: This document identifies contradictions between the Stellar Republic worldbuilding (master source) and the v5 Acts. The Stellar Republic is treated as canonical; the story needs revision to align.

---

## 1. CRITICAL: Citizenship System

### In Stellar Republic (MASTER):
The Republic **already has** a sophisticated tiered citizenship system that has existed since founding (2150-2300):

- **Non-Citizens** (~30%): No voting rights, legal protections, pay 15% tax
- **Junior Residents** (~15%): Children of citizens, ages 0-20, 12% tax, limited rights
- **Resident Citizens** (~35%): Completed 8 years service, vote locally, 10% tax
- **Full Citizens** (~20%): Completed 10-12 years service + examination, vote in all elections, hold any office

This is a **foundational feature** that has existed for 600 years.

### In Story v5 (INCONSISTENT):
Act II presents citizenship as **binary** (citizen vs non-citizen) and introduces "three-tier citizenship reform" as a **new political development**:

- Currently: 6 years military OR 12 years civilian → Federal Citizenship
- **Proposed Reform** (Act II, Week 26):
  - 2 years → Civic Participant (local voting)
  - 4 years → Regional Citizen (sector voting)
  - 6 years → Federal Citizen (full rights)

### THE PROBLEM:
The story treats tiered citizenship as a **new innovation** responding to the Webb security breach, when the Stellar Republic **already has** an even more sophisticated multi-tier system that's been in place for centuries.

### IMPACT:
This is the **major plot driver** for the detective subplot. The entire "citizenship reform in response to security vulnerabilities" narrative contradicts established worldbuilding.

## 2. Naming Convention: Confederation vs Republic

### In Stellar Republic (MASTER):
- Official name: "The Stellar Republic" or "Republic of Stars"
- Citizens call it "the Republic"
- Federal structure with Federal Senate, Federal Consuls, etc.
- Explicitly modeled on Roman Republic

### In Story v5 (INCONSISTENT):
- Called "the Confederation" throughout
- References "Confederation space," "Confederation Command," "Confederation military"
- Still uses Roman terminology (legions, centurions, etc.) but different political name

### RECOMMENDATION:
Change all references from "Confederation" to "Republic" or "Stellar Republic"

## 3. Timeline and Historical Context

### In Stellar Republic (MASTER):
Clear 600-year timeline:
- 2150-2165: Generation ships depart Earth
- 2280-2330: First landings
- 2300-2500: Isolation period
- 2500-2650: Reconnection and federation
- 2650-2750: Emergence as regional power
- **Present day: 2750**

Key historical markers:
- "First Generation" refers to the original colonists/founders (2150-2300)
- 150 years ago would be ~2600 (reconnection era)
- Federal structure formalized at Congress of Proxima (2640)

### In Story v5 (UNCLEAR):
- References "First Generation legionaries" 150 years ago
- Chen's grandfather served in "First Generation"
- Unclear what "First Generation" means
- No clear date or timeline placement
- "Confederation" founding ambiguous

### THE PROBLEM:
If the story is 150 years after founding, that would place it around 2300-2450 (isolation period), which contradicts:
- Advanced technology (jump drives, phase shields)
- Federal structure existence
- Multiple systems being connected
- Organized federal military

If "First Generation" means something else, it's unclear what.

### RECOMMENDATION:
- Clarify timeline placement (see Section 7 below for detailed recommendation)
- Redefine what "First Generation" means in story context
- Align with Stellar Republic historical periods

## 4. Service Requirements and Paths

### In Stellar Republic (MASTER):
**Clear service paths:**
- 8 years service in any branch → Resident Citizenship
- Additional 2-4 years + examination → Full Citizenship
- Five branches: Engineering Corps, Medical Service, Scientific Expedition, Civic Administration, Stellar Legion

**Federal Cursus Honorum** (established 2665):
1. Federal Academy (Ages 18-22, 4 years)
2. Junior Service (Ages 22-28, 6 years)
3. Quaestor (Age 28+)
4. Praetor (Age 36+)
5. System Senator (Age 40+)
6. Federal Senator (Age 45+)
7. Federal Consul (Age 50+)

### In Story v5 (INCONSISTENT):
- "6 years military OR 12 years civilian → Federal Citizenship"
- No mention of Resident vs Full Citizenship distinction
- No mention of examination requirement for Full Citizenship
- Service branches not clearly defined
- Chen described as promoted from ranks after 15 years—timeline unclear

### RECOMMENDATION:
Align service requirements with Stellar Republic's 8-year (Resident) and 10-12 year (Full) structure

## 5. Senate Structure and Composition

### In Stellar Republic (MASTER):
**Federal Senate:**
- Each member system sends 3 senators
- 15-year terms (accounting for light-speed communication delays)
- 42 member systems = 126 federal senators
- Elected by System Senates, not direct democracy

**System Senates:**
- 50 senators per system
- Elected from Full Citizens who completed service and reached age 30

**Federal Consuls:**
- Two executives elected by Federal Senate for 5-year terms
- Each can veto the other
- Command federal fleet

### In Story v5 (INCONSISTENT):
- Senate has 300 members
- Members are "former military commanders at cohort level or higher OR civilian administrators"
- "Lifelong membership after qualification"
- No mention of system representation
- Admiral Stone convenes emergency Senate session

### THE PROBLEM:
The story's Senate is a completely different institution:
- Different size (300 vs ~126)
- Different selection method (career qualification vs system election)
- Different term structure (lifelong vs 15 years)
- Doesn't represent federal structure

### RECOMMENDATION:
Either align with Federal Senate structure, or clarify this is a System Senate, not Federal Senate

## 6. Military Ranks and Structure

### In Stellar Republic (MASTER):
**Clear rank structure:**
- **Legate**: Commanding general of Legion (O-6)
- **Tribune**: Senior staff officers (O-4 to O-5)
- **Centurion**: Company commander (O-3), 30-80 personnel
- **Primus Pilus**: Senior centurion of First Cohort (E-9/O-5 equivalent)
- **Decanus**: 8-man squad leader
- **Optio**: Century second-in-command

**Legion structure:**
- 5,000-8,000 personnel total
- 10 Cohorts per Legion
- First Cohort is elite (200 marines)
- Cohorts II-X are line units (180-300 each)
- Centuries of ~30-80 personnel
- Contubernia of 8 soldiers

### In Story v5 (MOSTLY CONSISTENT BUT SOME ISSUES):
- Uses same terminology (Legate, Tribune, Centurion, etc.)
- 3rd Assault Legion: 5,000 personnel ✓
- **First Cohort: 800 personnel** (should be 200-300 per Stellar Republic)
- Cohorts II-X: 480 each (reasonable variation)
- Centurion Chen commands "a century of eighty legionaries" ✓
- Uses contubernium of 8 ✓

**Minor inconsistency**: First Cohort size (800 vs 200-300)

### RECOMMENDATION:
Adjust First Cohort size to align with traditional structure, or explain why 3rd Assault Legion has non-standard organization

## 8. Technology Inconsistencies

### Jump Drives
**In Story**:
- Jump drives described as "reverse-engineered from Krell technology"
- Used for FTL travel
- Can be destroyed by "emergency jump" overload

**In Stellar Republic**:
- Fusion-antimatter drives achieve 30-50% light speed
- No mention of jump drives or FTL
- Travel is subluminal, taking weeks to years between systems
- Communication relay stations every 5 light-years

### THE PROBLEM:
The story assumes FTL jump drives exist; Stellar Republic has only subluminal travel

### RECOMMENDATION:
Major decision needed:
1. Keep story's jump drives, add to Stellar Republic as recent breakthrough
2. Remove jump drives from story, adjust tactical situations for subluminal combat
3. Reconcile by having phase-shield/warp corridor tech be the path to FTL
